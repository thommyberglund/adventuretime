# SETTLEMENTS: THE LAST LIGHT AND THE CREEPING DARK

The coast is civilization's fist - clenched tight against the dark. But the further inland you go, the more the fingers loosen. The more the cracks show. The more the rot seeps through.

---

## **THE COASTAL BASTIONS: WHERE THE LIGHT STILL HOLDS**

### **Thornwall**
The jewel of the Shattered Coast. Forty feet of worked stone, garrisoned by the Lord Commander's finest. A city that has never fallen. A city that **will not fall**.

The temples here are **bright**. Their golden domes gleam in the sun, their priests' robes are white as fresh snow. The Order of the Dawn holds sway here, their faith unshaken, their power absolute. The people believe. The walls stand. The dark is **kept at bay**.

But even here, in the heart of civilization, there are cracks.

The Merchant Quarter still bears the scars of the First Warden's breach. Eleven missing. Multiple dead. Three days of containment, of locked doors and whispered prayers. The trading hall well still smells of **old blood** and something worse. The Iron Pact calls it "resolved." The survivors call it **a warning**.

And beneath the city, the Verath Tower stands in the old harbor. Four stories of ancient stone, built into the seawall. The scholars say it's just a relic. The fishermen say it **hums** at night.

**Notable locations within Thornwall:**

| Location | Description |
|---|---|
| The South Gate | Primary land entrance; sealed during the crisis |
| The Garrison Annex | Captain Undh's base of operations |
| The Merchant Quarter | Site of the Warden breach; sealed and then cleared |

**Terrain:** Rocky coastal headland. Stone walls. Deep harbor. Warm sea to the south.

### **Saltmere**
A younger sister to Thornwall, but no less defiant. The walls are lower but better maintained. The harbor is busy. The people are **watchful**.

The Order's temple here is new, its gold untarnished. The priests walk with confidence, their faith a shield against the dark. The Iron Pact's counting house is busy, trade flowing like water. The people are safe. The people are **protected**.

But the refugees keep coming. More than the city expected. More than it can handle. The northern roads are watched with **increasing alarm**. The garrison is stretched thin. The people whisper about the things that come down those roads at night.

And the harbor... the harbor is too quiet sometimes. The fishermen say the water is **colder** than it should be.

**Terrain:** Low headland. Coast road approach from the northeast. Harbor facing south. Lower walls than Thornwall but in better repair in places.

### **Thistle Hold**
A coastal settlement southwest of Saltmere. Built around a natural harbor where a river mouth has been dredged and walled against the tide. Old enough to have history, but not grand enough to have a garrison. Its defense rests on a town watch, a modest wall of timber and packed earth reinforced with stone at the gate, and the general hardiness of a population that has never known true safety.

The town's economy runs on fishing, coastal trade, and a modest market serving surrounding farmsteads. The people are suspicious of outsiders - not hostile, but watchful in the way of those who know trouble usually arrives on someone else's feet.

Beneath the town, a network of pre-settlement catacombs runs under the older districts. The townsfolk know they exist and avoid them. Occasionally something comes up from below, and the town watch handles it quietly and doesn't file reports.

**Notable locations:**

| Location | Description |
|---|---|
| The Abandoned Apothecary | Dilapidated building on the town's outskirts; formerly a working apothecary, now a front for necromantic operations |
| The Temple of the Dawn | Modest stone building near the town centre; seat of High Priestess Seraphine |
| The Iron Pact Counting House | A merchant association office on the harbor road; unofficial power broker in town commerce |
| The Catacombs | Pre-settlement tunnels beneath the older districts; origin unknown, largely unmapped |

**Terrain:** River mouth harbour. Timber and stone walls. Low coastal headland. Farmland to the north and east.

## **THE FRONTIER: WHERE THE DARKNESS CREEPS IN**

### **Henric's Mill**
A mill two leagues north of Ashford along the mill road, positioned where a reliable stream provides water power. Old Henric operated it before the recolonization wave and stayed when others left, giving the mill a permanence that newer settlements lack. The building is solid stone on the ground floor, timber above, with a defensible interior and a heavy door.

When Ashford fell, the miller led twelve survivors north along the mill road and fortified the mill as an emergency refuge. The mill's lantern served as a beacon visible from the road.

But the mill road is **too quiet** at night. The survivors from Ashford still jump at shadows. The granary smells of **rot**, though the grain is fresh. The stream sometimes runs **red**.

And the miller? Old Henric doesn't sleep much anymore. He says it's the noise of the wheel. But the wheel isn't turning at night.

**Terrain:** Rolling plains, one stream, a stand of birch to the east. Mill road runs north-south past the front.

### **Crale's Ford**
A village of approximately thirty souls, positioned where the mill road crosses a shallow ford in a seasonal river. The name suggests it was established by a founding family. When the undead threat spread south, the entire population organized and fled toward the coast in good order - doors left open, a pot still hanging over a cold hearth, chickens wandering the street.

No violence here. Just **absence**.

But the ford is still there. The stones still cross the river. And sometimes, when the wind is right, you can hear **voices** in the water.

**Terrain:** Shallow river crossing, ford with flat stones. Open plains on all sides. A dozen timber-and-thatch buildings around a central well.

### **Dunmore**
A village of eight buildings around a central well, positioned on the coast road between the frontier and Thornwall. The inn - the tallest building, two storeys - gave Bram a refuge when the village fell. The granary on the east side provided emergency shelter for children who arrived later.

Dunmore was hit harder than Crale's Ford. Six undead moved through it, killing or turning several residents before the survivors fled south. The eastern granary was where Ashford's children sheltered the night before Aldric arrived - they were directed north to Henric's Mill the following morning.

The well water tastes of **iron**. The coast road runs past, but no one stops anymore. The undead left marks. The buildings still stand, but the **shadows are wrong**. The children who sheltered in the granary are gone now. The ones who made it to Henric's Mill say they heard **singing** in the dark.

**Terrain:** Flat plains, coast road running north-south, low scrub to the east. Central well. Inn, granary, six smaller buildings.

### **Harrow Cross**
A village at a crossroads on the coast road, notable for the ruins of an old stone cross that gives it its name - a religious marker from an earlier period of settlement, the faith unclear. The eastern granary burned during the flight, its roof collapsing, though the main buildings survived. When Aldric passed through, the adult population had already fled south to Thornwall. A group of children had barred themselves in the surviving granary.

No one knows what happened to them.

The crossroads are empty now. The cross is **cracked**. The granary door is **scratched** on the inside. The Order calls it a tragedy. The Iron Pact calls it **an opportunity**.

**Terrain:** Crossroads. Open plains, low hills visible to the east. Collapsed granary on eastern edge. Smoke from the burned granary roof visible for several kilometers.

## **THE RUINS: WHERE THE PAST WON'T STAY BURIED**

### **Ashford**
A small colonial settlement on the open central plains, three seasons old at the time of the story. Built in the slow recolonization zone where the old empire's heartland is being cautiously resettled. Surrounded by flat grassland with no natural defenses beyond a timber palisade. The nearest village is several hours' travel in any direction.

Ashford sat at the edge of what locals call the safe zone - close enough to the mill road to receive supplies from the north, far enough into the plains that the dead were a nightly concern. It was overrun by six undead in a single night, its survivors fleeing north along the mill road to Henric's Mill.

The palisade is **broken** now. The well is **tainted**. The gatehouse torch post still stands, but the torch is **cold**. And sometimes, when the wind blows right, you can still hear the **screams**.

**Terrain:** Open plains, yellow grass, flat. Timber palisade wall. Single well. Gatehouse with torch post.

### **The Capital (Fallen)**
The seat of the Empire of the Sun, founded on a small village that grew over four thousand years into the greatest human city of its age. It fell to the undead armies when vampires drove the empire back from its southern borders and the capital was overrun before the last stand of the priesthood.

Its ruins stand in the central plains, a city of grand boulevards and imperial architecture slowly being reclaimed by grass and silence. The undead still walk its streets.

The boulevards are still there. The imperial architecture still stands, though the grass is **tall** and the silence is **deep**. The treasure hunters say the vaults are full. The survivors say the vaults are **hungry**. The Iron Pact says it's **none of their concern**.

**Terrain:** Central plains. Estimated to be built on a river confluence, now silted. Surrounding flatlands.

### **The Silent Cities (collective)**
The remains of the first human empire, predating the Empire of the Sun by thousands of years. Once built in a lush jungle-and-river landscape, the sorcerer-kings' blood rites consumed the land's life force until only desert remained. The cities stand half-buried in sand, their architecture vast and inhuman, their treasuries plundered only at the edges - the deep vaults remain unbreached.

Dragons nest in the outer structures, hoarding the gold of forgotten kings. The sorcerer-kings themselves lie entombed in the deepest chambers, waiting for the desert to bloom again.

The architecture is vast and **inhuman**. The treasuries are plundered only at the edges. The deep vaults remain **unbreached**. The desert is blooming again.

The Iron Pact says it's **bad for business** to go there. The Order says it's **heresy**. The survivors say it's **suicide**.

**Terrain:** Varies - deep desert, encroaching jungle, crumbling coastline. All hostile.

## **THE WILDERNESS: WHERE THE DARKNESS RULES**

### **Mishark**
A hidden ranger settlement located deep within the great forest, two days south of Thistle Hold. Comprised of sturdy wooden buildings, Mishark is known for its skilled hunters and trackers. It is a place of refuge for those exiled from more settled lands, where the people live in harmony with the forest and its creatures.

Or so they say.

The trees are **too quiet**. The animals watch with **human eyes**. The rangers don't talk about what they see in the deep woods. The settlement is safe. The settlement is **protected**. But the forest **remembers**. And the forest is **hungry**.

### **The Dwarven Mountain Kingdoms (collective)**
The dwarven mountain kingdoms carve their halls into the two great mountain ranges that flank the central plains. Their origins are lost to time, though legends speak of a great cataclysm that shattered their ancient forges. They trade iron, enchanted stone, and worked metal through caravan routes connecting to the Shattered Coast.

Their halls are deep. Their forges are hot. Their secrets are **many**.

The dwarves say their mountains are safe. The dwarves say their stone is **pure**. The dwarves say their ancestors sleep peacefully in the deep halls.

The miners say the **walls breathe**. The smiths say the metal **screams**. The exiles say the dwarves **lie**.

---
**Final Note**: The coast is safe. The temples are strong. The faith is bright. But the dark is **patient**. And the dark is **hungry**. The further you go from the sea, the thinner the light becomes. The more the rot shows. The more the past **claws its way back**.

The bastions will hold. For now. But the dark is **always there**. Waiting. Watching. **Hungry**.
