# A TAXONOMY OF THE DAMNED: CREATURES OF THE CURSED PLAINS
*Compiled by the Scholars of Thornwall, 912 TA*
*With field notes recovered from the Iron Pact's "Reclamation" Expeditions*
*Edited by Pol Edwick,¹ Former Scholar of Thornwall*

---

## **I. THE LESSER DEAD: A STUDY IN ATTRITION**

### **Classification and Origins**
The common undead—often referred to by the laity as "husks," "shamblers," or simply "the dead"—represent the lowest tier of necromantic reanimation. They are not a species, but a **symptom**. The soil of the Central Plains is so saturated with the ancient magic of the Sorcerer-Kings that any corpse left unburned will eventually rise.²

### **Physiology and Behavior**
The Lesser Dead exhibit a complete cessation of biological function, yet maintain locomotion through arcane suspension. Their physical degradation varies wildly. Some are little more than animated skeletons held together by dried sinew, while others are "fresh," still weeping fluids from their fatal wounds.

They possess no central nervous system, rendering them immune to pain, fatigue, or morale failure. Their primary drive is a mindless, insatiable hunger for the warmth of the living. They do not hunt with tactics; they hunt with **inevitability**.

**Combat Efficacy:**
Individually, a Lesser Dead is a trivial threat to a trained soldier. Their movements are jerky, their strikes uncoordinated. However, they are rarely encountered individually. They attack in swarms, utilizing a tactic scholars term "asphyxiation by mass." They do not defeat armies through martial superiority; they defeat them by exhausting the living until their weapons dull and their arms give out.³

**Scholarly Note:**
Some specimens exhibit residual behavioral loops—wearing the rusted bronze of the old empire, or attempting to perform tasks they completed in life. The Order of the Dawn claims this is the soul trapped in purgatory. Arcane scholars argue it is merely necrotic muscle memory. The Iron Pact simply notes that they are "an impediment to trade."

## **II. THE VAMPIRES: THE ARISTOCRACY OF THE GRAVE**

### **Classification and Origins**
If the Lesser Dead are the foot soldiers of the cursed plains, the vampires are its generals. They are not reanimated corpses; they are **ancient, apex predators**. Historical records indicate they are not native to the continent, but were awakened by the Empire of the Sun's necromancers as high servants of the entombed Sorcerer-Kings.⁴

### **Physiology and Behavior**
Vampires defy conventional biological and arcane categorization. They do not walk; they appear to **drift**, their physical forms capable of shifting like smoke to avoid strikes. Their flesh is cold, pale, and nearly impervious to mundane weaponry. A standard steel blade will often shatter against their skin before it cuts.

Unlike the mindless husks, vampires possess high intelligence, sentience, and a deeply ingrained cruelty. They do not kill efficiently. They kill **theatrically**. Field reports from surviving mercenaries describe them toying with seasoned veterans, allowing them to exhaust themselves before casually dismantling them.

**Combat Efficacy:**
A single vampire is classified as a catastrophic threat. They possess physical strength sufficient to crush a human windpipe with two fingers or shatter a ribcage with a casual backhand. They do not merely consume blood; they drain the ambient warmth and life force from their surroundings, turning flesh to dust with a touch.

**Vulnerabilities:**
They are notoriously difficult to destroy. Divine magic of the highest order can burn them, and concentrated, overwhelming arcane fire has proven effective, though the energy required usually kills the caster in the process.⁵

## **III. THE ORKS: AN ECOLOGY OF VIOLENCE**

### **Classification and Origins**
Orks (Colloquially: "Brutes," "Tusks," or "Greenskins") are native to the Northern Reaches and the Central Plains. Unlike the undead, they are biological entities—though "biological" is used loosely for a species that seems to thrive on injuries that would kill a horse. Elven records suggest the orks were the original inhabitants of the Boreal Belt before being driven into the fringes by elven expansion.

### **Physiology and Behavior**
An ork is a masterpiece of redundant systems. They possess high bone density, thick, leathery hides, and a metabolic rate that allows for near-instantaneous clotting. An ork does not "bleed out" in the traditional sense; it simply knits. This resilience is fueled by a massive caloric requirement, which explains their migratory raiding patterns.

They are not mindless. They possess a brutal, meritocratic social structure. Strength is the only currency they recognize, but they are capable of complex siege engineering and strategic retreats when the "Pack Impulse" dictates.

**Combat Efficacy:**
In close quarters, a single ork is a nightmare. They do not feel fear as humans do; their endocrine system treats pain as a stimulant. A veteran soldier might land three lethal strikes, only to be crushed by a dying ork's final, adrenaline-fueled spasm.

**Scholarly Note:**
The Iron Pact has attempted to negotiate "Labor Contracts" with northern tribes. The results were predictably bloody. The orks did not want the coin; they wanted the merchants.

## **IV. THE GOBLINS: THE VERMIN OF THE WASTES**

### **Classification and Origins**
Goblins are the parasitic shadows of the orkish tribes. Where there are orks, there are goblins—acting as scouts, scavengers, and, in lean winters, emergency rations. They breed with a terrifying fecundity that ensures their numbers never truly dwindle, no matter how many are slaughtered by frontier watchmen.

### **Physiology and Behavior**
Small, spindly, and possessed of a natural affinity for darkness, goblins rely on cowardice as their primary survival strategy. They do not fight fair. A goblin encounter begins with a poisoned dart from a thicket or a pit trap lined with rusted spikes. They are the only species that has successfully weaponized the "tactical whimpering" to lure soft-hearted recruits into ambushes.

**Combat Efficacy:**
Individual goblins are pathetic. Ten goblins are a nuisance. Fifty goblins are a death sentence. They utilize a "swarm-and-stab" methodology, focusing on hamstrings and throat-slitting. They are also remarkably adept at jury-rigging machinery; a goblin-built catapult is twice as likely to explode as a dwarven one, but it's half the price and twice as messy.

**Scholarly Note:**
Some suggest goblins possess a shared, low-level psychic link that allows them to coordinate in the dark. Pol Edwick argued they're just "exceptionally good at screaming in unison."

## **V. THE ELVES: THE DYING ECHOES**

### **Classification and Origins**
Elves claim to be the first children of the stars, arriving on the continent during the Great Conjunction of the moons. While the bards sing of their timeless beauty, the reality is far more clinical. They are a civilization in terminal decline, clinging to the Sylvan Deep like a drowning man to a rock.

### **Physiology and Behavior**
Elves possess a biological imperative for "static peace." They do not age in the human sense; instead, they "fade." As they grow older, their skin becomes translucent and their heart rate slows until they eventually petrify or simply cease to be. This slow metabolism makes them remarkably patient but utterly resistant to cultural or technological change.

They view the shorter-lived races with a mixture of pity and profound irritation. To an elf, a human life is a frantic, messy blink of an eye.

**Combat Efficacy:**
Elves prioritize precision over raw power. Their archers are capable of feats that defy physics, often attributed to a low-level, instinctive manipulation of air currents. They do not seek conquest; they seek the removal of "impurities" from their sacred groves.

**Scholarly Note:**
The mainland elves are effectively an endangered species. Every warrior they lose is a library burned. Pol Edwick noted that they are the only race that treats a tactical victory as a funeral.

## **VI. THE DWARVES: THE MASTERS OF THE DEEP**

### **Classification and Origins**
Dwarves are the stone in the world’s foundation. While humans squabble over dirt, the dwarves claim the bones of the earth. Their origin is a subject of intense dwarven secrecy—the "Vaults of Life" mentioned in their texts have never been found by outsiders, leading some to suspect they are a purely artificial species.

### **Physiology and Behavior**
Dwarves are masterpieces of durability. Their high bone density and thick musculature make them nearly twice as heavy as a human of comparable height. Over centuries, their skin often absorbs mineral deposits from their environment, giving them a literal stony complexion.

Their society is a rigid hierarchy of guilds and clans. They are the masters of the "long contract," often holding human families in debt for generations.

**Combat Efficacy:**
A dwarven phalanx is a mobile fortress. They utilize heavy, overlapping shields and short, brutal polearms. They do not retreat; they simply wait for the enemy to break against them. Their mastery of enchanted stone also allows them to utilize siege engines that function with terrifying, clockwork reliability.

**Scholarly Note:**
The rumor that dwarves grind their ancestors into "enchanted stone" remains unproven, largely because the dwarves execute anyone found asking too many questions in their mines.

## **VII. THE FORGOTTEN: WHAT THE SCHOLARS DON'T TELL YOU**

### **The Hollowborn**
A rare and poorly documented variant of the Lesser Dead, the Hollowborn are not reanimated corpses. They are **empty vessels**—living humans who have been hollowed out from within, their bodies left standing, their minds erased. They do not attack. They do not hunger. They simply **stand**, staring with blank eyes, until something living comes close enough to **fill the void**.

**Field Observations:**
- No recorded instances of Hollowborn attacking or defending themselves.
- They appear to **absorb** living beings who approach too closely, though the mechanism is unknown.
- The Iron Pact has classified them as "non-hostile" and "potentially useful for labor." The Order of the Dawn has declared them "demonically possessed."

**Scholarly Consensus:**
There is none. The Hollowborn are either a failed experiment in necromancy or the result of some forgotten sorcerer-king's cruelty. The Iron Pact would prefer to **sell them**. The Order would prefer to **burn them**. The survivors just want them **gone**.

---

## **VIII. CONCLUSION: THE FUTILITY OF CLASSIFICATION**

Studying the dead is a luxury of those sitting behind forty feet of stone in Thornwall. Out on the plains, taxonomy is irrelevant. Whether a creature is driven by necrotic muscle memory or ancient, sentient malice, the end result is the same: they want to eat you, and they have all of eternity to try.
