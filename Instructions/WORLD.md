# A COMPREHENSIVE HISTORY OF THE KNOWN WORLD
*Compiled by the Scholars of Thornwall, 912 TA*
*With contributions from the Iron Pact Archives and the Dwarven Mountain Kingdoms*
*Edited by Pol Edwick,¹ Former Scholar of Thornwall*

---

## **I. GEOGRAPHY AND CLIMATE: A LAND THAT HATES YOU**

### **The Northern Reaches**
The Boreal Belt is a vast expanse of evergreen forests, stretching from the Frozen North to the Central Plains. Dwarven records (see *The Stone Chronicles*, Vol. 47) describe it as a "land of silence," though this is likely a euphemism for "a place where screams go unheard."² The Sylvan Deep, a subregion of the Belt, is home to the last mainland elves—a reclusive people who, according to elven oral tradition, have not expanded their territory in over 2,000 years.

**Scholarly Debate**: The lack of elven expansion is often attributed to their "static peace" philosophy, though some historians (notably Edwick, 899 TA) argue it reflects a **civilizational decline**.³

---

### **The Central Plains**
The Central Plains are a patchwork of feudal kingdoms, merchant cities, and ruins. The climate is temperate, with hot summers and mild winters—ideal for agriculture, were it not for the **undead incursions** and **ork raiding parties** that plague the region. The Iron Pact's *Trade Ledgers* (905 TA) note that 60% of the region's grain is controlled by three noble families, who are, unsurprisingly, the same families that control the region's armies.

**Key Features**:
- **The Feudal Corridors**: A network of roads and fortified towns, built atop the ruins of older civilizations. The roads are well-maintained, though this is less a sign of progress and more a sign of the Iron Pact's **stranglehold on trade**.
- **The Wizard's Spires**: Isolated towers where arcane scholars study the "Whispers of the Forgotten" (see *MAGIC.md*). The towers are universally avoided by peasants, who believe them to be cursed. They are not wrong.⁴
- **The Necromantic Hollows**: A series of catacombs beneath the plains, where necromancers are said to "harvest" the dead. The Order of the Dawn has repeatedly petitioned the Iron Pact to seal the Hollows, but the Pact has refused, citing "economic interests" (Iron Pact Memo #47-B, 908 TA).⁵

**The Empire of the Sun**:
The Empire of the Sun did not fall to barbarians or monsters. It fell to its own rot. The nobles sold the grain stores to the Iron Pact. The priests hoarded the last holy relics. The emperor slit his own throat rather than face the mob. The undead simply walked in afterward. No grand battle. Just cleanup.

**Post-Collapse**:
The empire's fall left a power vacuum. The coastal cities, led by Thornwall and Saltmere, formed the **Shattered Coast Alliance**, while the Iron Pact consolidated control over trade routes. The recolonization of the plains began in 890 TA, though progress has been slow. As of 912 TA, only 12% of the empire's former territory has been reclaimed (Iron Pact Survey, 911 TA). The Iron Pact calls this "progress." The widows call it "another empty bed."

## **II. THE FIRST EMPIRES: WHERE IT ALL WENT WRONG**

### **The Sorcerer-Kings and the Desolation**
The First Empires arose in what is now the southern deserts. Archaeological evidence (see *The Silent Cities Excavations*, 887 TA) suggests a **highly advanced society**, though "advanced" is a relative term. Their greatest achievement was **draining the life from the land itself** to fuel their magic.

**The Blood Rites**:
The First Empires practiced **human sacrifice on an industrial scale**. The *Annals of the Iron Pact* (Vol. 3) describe "pyramids of skulls" in the Silent Cities, some containing **tens of thousands of remains**. The sacrifices were not merely religious—they fueled the Sorcerer-Kings' magic, which **drained the land itself**. By 3000 BA, the jungles had withered into desert, and the Sorcerer-Kings, facing starvation, **entombed themselves** to await the desert's rebirth.

**Modern Implications**:
The desert has begun to bloom in recent decades, a phenomenon some scholars (e.g., Pol Edwick, 901 TA) attribute to the Sorcerer-Kings' return. Others argue it is a **natural cycle** (see *The Desert's Breath*, a dwarven geological study, 905 TA). The Iron Pact has **banned further excavation** of the Silent Cities, citing "safety concerns."⁶

---

### **The Elves: A Civilization in Decline (Or Just Really Bad at Change)**
The elves arrived in the world **concurrently with the three moons**, according to their own myths. Early elven texts (e.g., *The Book of the First Dawn*) describe them as **conquerors**, slaughtering the native orks and goblins to claim the forests. However, by 1000 BA, the elves had **fractured** in a civil war that left their empire in ruins.

**The Elven Paradox**:
Elven philosophy revolves around **"static peace"**—a rejection of change. This has led to **cultural stagnation**. While their island cousins remain isolationist, the mainland elves have **retreated into the Sylvan Deep**, where they guard "eternal groves" that, according to elven priests, **prevent the world's natural cycles from accelerating**. Some human scholars (notably Edwick) argue this is **delusional**, while others (e.g., the Order of the Dawn) believe the elves are **unwittingly delaying the Sorcerer-Kings' return**.⁷

---

### **The Dwarves: A Mystery in Stone (And Probably Other Things)**
The dwarves claim **no origin myth**. Their oldest texts (e.g., *The First Forges*) speak of "vaults of life" that turned stone into flesh, but no such vaults have ever been found. They emerged in the mountains around 2000 BA, fully formed as a **trading civilization**.

**Economic Power**:
The dwarves control the world's **iron and enchanted stone** trade. The Iron Pact's ledgers show that **80% of all weapons** in the Shattered Coast are dwarven-made. However, rumors persist that their "enchanted stone" is **ground from petrified ancestors** (see *The Stone Trade Controversy*, a banned text by the Order of the Dawn, 903 TA). The dwarves deny this, of course.⁸

---

## **III. THE RISE AND FALL OF HUMANITY: A CAUTIONARY TALE**

### **The Kwa-Hazalits and the Fate**
The first humans, the **Kwa-Hazalits**, arrived from the east via a now-submerged land bridge. They brought with them **The Fate**—a belief system that posits a **divine plan** for humanity. The Fate's central tenet is that **belief itself is power**, a claim that has led to **centuries of conflict** with arcane scholars. The arcane scholars, for their part, call it **"a load of superstitious nonsense."**⁹

**The New Empire**:
The Empire of the Sun began as a **single village** in the Central Plains. Over 4,000 years, it expanded into a **continental power**, only to collapse in 872 TA. The empire's fall is often blamed on the **undead and vampires**, but contemporary accounts (e.g., *The Last Emperor's Journal*) reveal a **decayed, corrupt regime** that could not adapt. The emperor's own guard deserted in the final days, and the capital's gates were left unbarred. The undead did not conquer the city. They simply **walked in**.

**The Recolonization**:
The recolonization of the plains began in 890 TA, led by the **Shattered Coast Alliance**. Progress has been **slow and bloody**. The Iron Pact's *Casualty Reports* (911 TA) list **12,000 dead** in the past 22 years, with **undead and orks** accounting for 60% of losses. The Iron Pact calls this "progress." The widows call it "another empty bed." The undead call it "dinner."

## **IV. MODERN THREATS: OR, WHY YOU SHOULDN'T GET TOO COMFORTABLE**

### **The Undead and the Vampires**
The undead are not a **monolithic threat**. Some are **mindless husks**, while others retain **fragments of their former selves**. The vampires, meanwhile, are **not native to this world**—they are the **minions of the Sorcerer-Kings**, awakened by the empire's necromancers.

**The Last Stand of the Priests**:
The priests' final act in 872 TA is **shrouded in myth**. Some texts (e.g., *The Eternal Day*) claim the sun **stopped in the sky for ten years**, while others (e.g., *The Order's Secret Histories*) argue it was a **mass hallucination**. The truth is likely more mundane: the priests lit every torch they had and prayed while the vampires picked them off one by one. The survivors called it a miracle. The dead called it dinner.¹⁰

---

### **The Iron Pact: A Shadow Government (And Not a Very Subtle One)**
The Iron Pact is **not a kingdom**, but it **controls kingdoms**. Its power lies in **debt, contracts, and information**. In smaller settlements like Thistle Hold, the Pact is the **de facto government**, though it **denies any official authority**.

**Criticism**:
The Order of the Dawn has accused the Pact of **exploiting the recolonization** for profit. The Pact's response: *"We provide a service. If people choose to pay for it, that is their affair."* (Iron Pact Memo #89-C, 909 TA). The Pact's critics call this **"predatory lending."** The Pact calls it **"business."**

---

### **The Sorcerer-Kings' Return: A Problem for Future Generations (Probably)**
The desert's bloom is **either a blessing or a curse**. If the Sorcerer-Kings return, the world will face a threat **unlike any other**. The elves' "static peace" may be the only thing holding them back—or it may be **exactly what they need to wake**. Scholars debate which would be worse: the Sorcerer-Kings' return or the Iron Pact's inevitable attempt to monetize it.

**Scholarly Consensus**:
There is **none**. The Order of the Dawn preaches vigilance, the Iron Pact **bans discussion of the topic**, and the dwarves **refuse to comment**. The only consensus is that **no one knows what's coming**, and **no one wants to find out**.

---

## **V. CONCLUSION: A WORLD ON THE BRINK (AND NO ONE CARES)**
The known world is a **fragile thing**. The recolonization of the plains is **far from assured**, the elves are **in decline**, and the Sorcerer-Kings **wait beneath the sand**. The only certainty is that **change is coming**—whether humanity is ready or not.

**Final Note**:
This text is **not exhaustive**. Many records have been lost, and many truths remain **buried**. The scholars of Thornwall will continue to **revise and expand** this history as new evidence emerges. Assuming, of course, that the undead don't eat us first.
