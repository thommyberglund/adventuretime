# A COMPREHENSIVE HISTORY OF THE KNOWN WORLD
*Compiled by the Scholars of Thornwall, 912 TA*
*With contributions from the Iron Pact Archives and the Dwarven Mountain Kingdoms*

---

## **I. GEOGRAPHY AND CLIMATE**

### **The Northern Reaches**
The Boreal Belt, a vast expanse of evergreen forests, stretches from the Frozen North to the Central Plains. Dwarven records (see *The Stone Chronicles*, Vol. 47) describe it as a "land of silence," where the only sounds are the wind through the pines and the distant hammering of mountain forges. The Sylvan Deep, a subregion of the Belt, is home to the last mainland elves—a reclusive people who, according to elven oral tradition, have not expanded their territory in over 2,000 years.

**Scholarly Debate**: The lack of elven expansion is often attributed to their "static peace" philosophy, though some historians (notably Edwick, 899 TA) argue it reflects a **civilizational decline**.

---

### **The Central Plains**
The heartland of humanity, the Central Plains are a patchwork of feudal kingdoms, merchant cities, and ruins. The climate is temperate, with hot summers and mild winters—ideal for agriculture, were it not for the **undead incursions** and **ork raiding parties** that plague the region.

**Key Features**:
- **The Feudal Corridors**: A network of roads and fortified towns, built atop the ruins of older civilizations. The Iron Pact’s *Trade Ledgers* (905 TA) note that 60% of the region’s grain is controlled by three noble families.
- **The Wizard’s Spires**: Isolated towers where arcane scholars study the "Whispers of the Forgotten" (see *MAGIC.md*). The towers are universally avoided by peasants, who believe them to be cursed.
- **The Necromantic Hollows**: A series of catacombs beneath the plains, where necromancers are said to "harvest" the dead. The Order of the Dawn has repeatedly petitioned the Iron Pact to seal the Hollows, but the Pact has refused, citing "economic interests" (Iron Pact Memo #47-B, 908 TA).

**The Empire of the Sun**:
The last great human empire, the Empire of the Sun, collapsed in 872 TA after a century of decline. Contemporary accounts (e.g., *The Last Days of the Sun*, a priestly chronicle) describe a **rot from within**: nobles hoarding grain, priests selling indulgences, and the emperor’s own guard deserting in the final days. The capital fell not to a grand battle, but to **neglect**—the gates were left unbarred, and the undead simply walked in.

**Post-Collapse**:
The empire’s fall left a power vacuum. The coastal cities, led by Thornwall and Saltmere, formed the **Shattered Coast Alliance**, while the Iron Pact consolidated control over trade routes. The recolonization of the plains began in 890 TA, though progress has been slow. As of 912 TA, only 12% of the empire’s former territory has been reclaimed (Iron Pact Survey, 911 TA).

## **II. THE FIRST EMPIRES**

### **The Sorcerer-Kings and the Desolation**
The oldest known civilization, the **First Empires**, arose in what is now the southern deserts. Archaeological evidence (see *The Silent Cities Excavations*, 887 TA) suggests a **highly advanced society**, with monumental architecture and a written language yet to be deciphered.

**The Blood Rites**:
The First Empires practiced **human sacrifice on an industrial scale**. The *Annals of the Iron Pact* (Vol. 3) describe "pyramids of skulls" in the Silent Cities, some containing **tens of thousands of remains**. The sacrifices were not merely religious—they fueled the Sorcerer-Kings’ magic, which **drained the land itself**. By 3000 BA (Before the Age), the jungles had withered into desert, and the Sorcerer-Kings, facing starvation, **entombed themselves** to await the desert’s rebirth.

**Modern Implications**:
The desert has begun to bloom in recent decades, a phenomenon some scholars (e.g., Pol Edwick, 901 TA) attribute to the Sorcerer-Kings’ return. Others argue it is a **natural cycle** (see *The Desert’s Breath*, a dwarven geological study, 905 TA). The Iron Pact has **banned further excavation** of the Silent Cities, citing "safety concerns."

---

### **The Elves: A Civilization in Decline**
The elves arrived in the world **concurrently with the three moons**, according to their own myths. Early elven texts (e.g., *The Book of the First Dawn*) describe them as **conquerors**, slaughtering the native orks and goblins to claim the forests. However, by 1000 BA, the elves had **fractured** in a civil war that left their empire in ruins.

**The Elven Paradox**:
Elven philosophy revolves around **"static peace"**—a rejection of change. This has led to **cultural stagnation**. While their island cousins remain isolationist, the mainland elves have **retreated into the Sylvan Deep**, where they guard "eternal groves" that, according to elven priests, **prevent the world’s natural cycles from accelerating**. Some human scholars (notably Edwick) argue this is **delusional**, while others (e.g., the Order of the Dawn) believe the elves are **unwittingly delaying the Sorcerer-Kings’ return**.

---

### **The Dwarves: A Mystery in Stone**
The dwarves claim **no origin myth**. Their oldest texts (e.g., *The First Forges*) speak of "vaults of life" that turned stone into flesh, but no such vaults have ever been found. They emerged in the mountains around 2000 BA, fully formed as a **trading civilization**.

**Economic Power**:
The dwarves control the world’s **iron and enchanted stone** trade. The Iron Pact’s ledgers show that **80% of all weapons** in the Shattered Coast are dwarven-made. However, rumors persist that their "enchanted stone" is **ground from petrified ancestors** (see *The Stone Trade Controversy*, a banned text by the Order of the Dawn, 903 TA).

## **III. THE RISE AND FALL OF HUMANITY**

### **The Kwa-Hazalits and the Fate**
The first humans, the **Kwa-Hazalits**, arrived from the east via a now-submerged land bridge. They brought with them **The Fate**—a belief system that posits a **divine plan** for humanity. The Fate’s central tenet is that **belief itself is power**, a claim that has led to **centuries of conflict** with arcane scholars.

**The New Empire**:
The Empire of the Sun began as a **single village** in the Central Plains. Over 4,000 years, it expanded into a **continental power**, only to collapse in 872 TA. The empire’s fall is often blamed on the **undead and vampires**, but contemporary accounts (e.g., *The Last Emperor’s Journal*) reveal a **decayed, corrupt regime** that could not adapt.

**The Recolonization**:
The recolonization of the plains began in 890 TA, led by the **Shattered Coast Alliance**. Progress has been **slow and bloody**. The Iron Pact’s *Casualty Reports* (911 TA) list **12,000 dead** in the past 22 years, with **undead and orks** accounting for 60% of losses.

---

## **IV. MODERN THREATS AND UNCERTAINTIES**

### **The Undead and the Vampires**
The undead are not a **monolithic threat**. Some are **mindless husks**, while others retain **fragments of their former selves**. The vampires, meanwhile, are **not native to this world**—they are the **minions of the Sorcerer-Kings**, awakened by the empire’s necromancers.

**The Last Stand of the Priests**:
The priests’ final act in 872 TA is **shrouded in myth**. Some texts (e.g., *The Eternal Day*) claim the sun **stopped in the sky for ten years**, while others (e.g., *The Order’s Secret Histories*) argue it was a **mass hallucination**. The truth may never be known.

### **The Iron Pact: A Shadow Government**
The Iron Pact is **not a kingdom**, but it **controls kingdoms**. Its power lies in **debt, contracts, and information**. In smaller settlements like Thistle Hold, the Pact is the **de facto government**, though it **denies any official authority**.

**Criticism**:
The Order of the Dawn has accused the Pact of **exploiting the recolonization** for profit. The Pact’s response: *"We provide a service. If people choose to pay for it, that is their affair."* (Iron Pact Memo #89-C, 909 TA).

### **The Sorcerer-Kings’ Return**
The desert’s bloom is **either a blessing or a curse**. If the Sorcerer-Kings return, the world will face a threat **unlike any other**. The elves’ "static peace" may be the only thing holding them back—or it may be **exactly what they need to wake**.

**Scholarly Consensus**:
There is **none**. The Order of the Dawn preaches vigilance, the Iron Pact **bans discussion of the topic**, and the dwarves **refuse to comment**.

---

## **V. CONCLUSION: A WORLD ON THE BRINK**
The known world is a **fragile thing**. The recolonization of the plains is **far from assured**, the elves are **in decline**, and the Sorcerer-Kings **wait beneath the sand**. The only certainty is that **change is coming**—whether humanity is ready or not.

**Final Note**:
This text is **not exhaustive**. Many records have been lost, and many truths remain **buried**. The scholars of Thornwall will continue to **revise and expand** this history as new evidence emerges.
