# THE COST OF POWER: A TREATISE ON MAGIC'S TRUE PRICE

Magic is not a gift. It is a **transaction**. Every spell exacts payment — sometimes immediately, sometimes slowly, but always inevitably. The world does not give power freely. It **takes**.

And sometimes — just sometimes — it **gives back**. But never without interest.

---
## **I. ARCANE MAGIC: THE WORLD'S HUNGER**

Arcane magic is the raw force of creation, and creation is **never free**. Those who wield it do not command power — they **borrow** it, and the world always collects its due.

Rest can reverse the damage. A fire mage's blistered hands may heal. A water mage's fluid-filled lungs may clear. An earth mage's petrified joints may soften. An air mage's fading voice may return.

The debt remains. And the interest grows.

### **Elemental Magic: The Fourfold Curse**

The elements do not serve. They **consume**.

**Fire Magic** starts with reddened palms. A sting, easily ignored. After a hundred spells, the blisters come — deep ones, the kind that weep clear fluid and split when you grip a staff. After a thousand, the hands are fused to the wood, blackened flesh peeling away like charred parchment. A week of rest might heal the blisters. A month might restore the hands. The scars remain. The next casting burns deeper. Fire mages do not die of old age. They burn out long before.

**Water Magic** fills the lungs with brine after each casting. A cough at first. A wheeze. A week of prayer to the river gods might clear the fluid. A month might restore the breath. The salt lingers in the veins, and the next spell fills the lungs faster. The Order calls this "divine communion." The drowned call it suffocation.

**Earth Magic** hardens the bones. Stiffens the joints. A week of meditation in the deep caves might soften the stone. A month might restore movement. The weight remains, and the next casting turns more bone to rock. The dwarves praise this as "becoming one with the stone." The petrified know it as entombment.

**Air Magic** takes the voice first. Then the breath. A week of silence in the high mountains might restore the voice. A month might return the breath. The emptiness remains, and the next spell steals more air. The last air mage of Saltmere stood silent as the noose tightened, her vocal cords long since turned to dust.

The elements will take, but they will also give back — for a time. The interest compounds. The elements always collect in the end.

---
## **II. DIVINE MAGIC: THE GODS' GIFT**

Divine magic is not a transaction. It is a **blessing**. The gods do not take. They give. Their power flows pure and bright, a beacon in the dark. The priests' hands do not blacken. The protectors' skin does not harden. The summoners' voices do not fade.

The gods are generous. Their chosen are protected. Their miracles are real.

### **Healing Magic: The Miracle**

The priest's hands remain clean. The sick rise, whole and healed. The wounds close without scar. The pain fades like morning mist.

The first healing brings light. The patient's breath steadies. Their color returns. The priest glows with divine favor. The hundredth healing brings wonder — the lame walk, the blind see, the priest is called blessed. The thousandth brings awe. The dying live. The dead return. The priest is called saint.

*Somewhere, a child coughs blood into their pillow. Somewhere, a mother's milk runs dry. Somewhere, a farmer's crops wither overnight. The gods do not take from the priest. They take from those who cannot fight back. The Order calls this "divine balance." The afflicted call it a curse. But the afflicted are not the ones writing the texts.*

### **Protection Magic: The Shield**

The protector stands unharmed. Arrows shatter against their skin. Blades turn aside. Fire will not burn them. Their faith is rewarded. The gods are with them.

*Somewhere, a soldier's armor rusts overnight. Somewhere, a village's palisade rots to dust. Somewhere, a king's fortress crumbles without warning. The gods do not weaken the protector. They weaken those who trust in stone and steel. The Order calls this "divine will." The ruined call it betrayal. But the ruined are not the ones with temples.*

### **Summoning Magic: The Angels**

The summoner calls, and the angels come. Radiant. Powerful. Merciful. Their light drives back the dark. Their sword smites the wicked. Their voice shakes the heavens. The summoner is called prophet.

*Somewhere, a demon slips its chains. Somewhere, a saint falls to temptation. Somewhere, a soldier's sword hand trembles at the wrong moment. The gods do not corrupt the summoner. They release corruption elsewhere. The Order calls this "divine justice." The damned call it abandonment. But the damned have already stopped praying.*

---
The priest's hands stay clean. The protector's skin stays whole. The summoner's voice stays strong. The gods give freely to their chosen.

They simply take from someone else. And they never run out of someones.

---
## **III. FORBIDDEN MAGIC: THE ABYSS' BARGAINS**

Forbidden magic is not a path. It is a **plunge**. Those who practice it do not walk away. They are taken, piece by piece. The abyss does not give back.

### **Necromancy: The Rot's Embrace**

The first casting leaves the necromancer's teeth slightly sensitive. Barely noticed. A sacrifice might numb the pain. A life might delay what's coming. But the decay begins, and it will not stop.

By the hundredth casting, the gums bleed. The breath stinks of the grave. No amount of blood reverses this — it only slows it. By the final casting, the flesh is sloughing away, the bones visible beneath papery skin. The dead do not care. They take what they are owed.

There is no rest. No reversal. Only sacrifice — and the sacrifice must be greater each time. A drop of blood. A finger. A life. The necromancer can delay the rot. They cannot escape it.

### **Blood Magic: The Butcher's Ledger**

Blood magic is simple arithmetic: life in, power out.

The first casting takes a drop. A pinprick. The wound heals. The ledger is open, and it will not close. The thousandth casting takes a vein — a slow, deliberate cut. No rest reverses this. The blood demands more, and the caster's flesh begins to wither. The final casting takes everything, and the caster finds themselves on the altar instead of behind it.

The blood does not care whose it is. But it will be paid.

### **Shadow Magic: The Void's Embrace**

The first casting leaves the shadow slightly wrong. It returns to normal — for a time. The void remembers, and the next casting twists it further.

By the hundredth casting, the shadow moves on its own. No light reverses this. The void claims more, and the caster's substance begins to fade. The final casting leaves the caster without a shadow, their substance fading like mist in sunlight.

The void does not give back. It only takes.

---
## **IV. THE MAGIC IN THE WORLD: A BALANCE OF SUFFERING**

Magic exists. It works. And sometimes, it can be undone.

But the reversal is never complete. The scars remain. The debt grows. The cost is always higher than before.

In the settlements, the Iron Pact sells "restorative charms" that do something, but never enough. The Order sells "redemptive prayers" that ease the burden but never remove it. The people buy them anyway, because the alternative is worse.

In the wild, the forest might forgive a transgression. The mountains might spare a climber. The sea might return what it took. But the world always remembers, and the next offense will be punished more severely.

In the people:
- The clerics bear their blackened veins, praying for relief that comes slower each time.
- The mages hide their scars, knowing the next spell will cut deeper.
- The necromancers do not recover. They only delay.
- And all of them, when the end comes, wonder if it was worth it.

Magic is not a tool. It is a **creditor**. It gives what you ask, and then it takes what it wants. The small prices can sometimes be reversed. The debts can sometimes be lessened.

The interest always grows. The final cost is always paid in full.

If you seek power, know this — you will find it. And it will find you in return. The gods may forgive. The elements may relent. But the dark arts? They never let go.
