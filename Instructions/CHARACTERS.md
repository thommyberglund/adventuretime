# CHARACTERS: THE BROKEN AND THE DAMNED

These are not heroes. They are **survivors**. Some are better than others. Most are worse. All of them are carrying something that will eventually break them.

---

## **ELDRIN OF MISHARK: THE RANGER WHO SEES TOO MUCH**

**Appearance:**
Tall, lean, and weathered like old leather. His hair is the color of autumn leaves, tied back in a practical braid that's starting to fray at the edges. His eyes are sharp and watchful, the hue of a forest stream - or so the bards say. In truth, they're the color of **someone who has seen things he can't unsee**. His leather armor is well-maintained but shows the scars of too many close calls. The longbow he carries is dark yew wood, its string worn from use. His hands are steady, but his sleep isn't.

**Skills and Abilities:**
- **Expert archer** with unerring accuracy (when it matters)
- **Skilled tracker** who can find what doesn't want to be found
- **Knowledgeable about forest lore** (and which plants will kill you slowly)
- **Proficient with twin short swords** (for when the arrows run out)

**Personality:**
Quiet. Observant. The kind of man who speaks only when necessary, which is **more often than he'd like**. He respects nature in the way a man respects a wolf - he knows it's beautiful, he knows it's necessary, and he knows it will **tear his throat out** if he's not careful.

His loyalty is fierce, but it's **not free**. He's seen what happens to those who trust too easily. The forest taught him that lesson with **blood and screams**.

**Backstory:**
Eldrin grew up in the wilds of Mishark, where the trees watch you and the animals have **human eyes**. His father was a ranger, or so the story goes. The truth is, his father was a man who knew which way to run when the shadows got too long. Eldrin learned that lesson well.

When his village was attacked by bandits, he didn't stay to fight. He **ran**, like his father taught him. Now he tells himself it was to seek justice. The truth is simpler: he was afraid. He still is. But he's better at hiding it now.

**The Truth:**
Eldrin doesn't protect people because it's right. He protects them because he's **terrified** of being alone in the dark again. His arrows fly true, but his hands shake when no one's looking. He's not a hero. He's a man who knows the cost of failure, and he's **not willing to pay it again**.

## **VESPATIAN: THE CLERIC WHO BELIEVES (AND THAT'S THE PROBLEM)**

**Appearance:**
Average height. Stout build. The kind of man who looks like he was designed to be **comforting**. His eyes are a deep brown, the kind that make people want to confess their sins. He wears simple robes of white and gold, the symbol of his god prominently displayed on a pendant around his neck. His hands are calloused from years of wielding a mace, but his smile is still soft. Too soft.

**Skills and Abilities:**
- **Skilled healer** who can mend wounds (but not souls)
- **Proficient in spells of light and protection** (which never seem to be enough)
- **Experienced with a mace** (he's better at healing than hurting, but he's had to do both)
- **Knowledgeable about religious lore** (and the lies his Order tells)

**Personality:**
Kind. Compassionate. The kind of man who would give a stranger his last coin. His voice is gentle, his manner soothing. He believes in his god with a fervor that is **either inspiring or terrifying**, depending on who you ask.

But there's a **crack** in his faith. It's small, but it's there. He's seen too many miracles that weren't. He's healed too many wounds that shouldn't have been there in the first place. His kindness is real, but his certainty is **fraying at the edges**.

**Backstory:**
Vespatian was raised in the temple of his god, trained to be a cleric from a young age. He believed in the light, in the goodness of his Order, in the righteousness of his cause. Then he saw his temple **burn**.

The priests told him it was a test. The survivors told him it was a **warning**. Vespatian doesn't know which is true. But he knows his hands were the last ones to touch the dying, and that's a weight that doesn't **wash off**.

**The Truth:**
Vespatian's healing magic leaves **black veins** creeping up his arms. He tells himself it's the cost of service. The truth is, he doesn't know if his god is listening anymore. He still prays. He still heals. But every time he closes his eyes, he sees the faces of those he **couldn't save**.

His mace is heavy. His faith is **heavier**.

## **GARRICK THE UNBROKEN: THE MERCENARY WHO BROKE HIMSELF FIRST**

**Appearance:**
Tall. Muscular. The kind of man who looks like he was carved from a block of **cold, unyielding stone**. A scar runs diagonally across his face, a reminder of a battle he **shouldn't have survived**. His dark hair falls to his shoulders, his eyes are the color of a blade in moonlight. He wears practical leather armor, reinforced with metal plates - the kind of armor that says "I expect to be hit, and I expect to hit back harder." His rapier is finely crafted, its edge **always sharp**.

**Skills and Abilities:**
- **Master of the rapier** with reflexes like a striking snake
- **Skilled in hand-to-hand combat** (he prefers steel, but his fists work fine)
- **Experienced in tracking and survival** (he knows how to find people, and how to **make them disappear**)
- **Knowledgeable about battle strategies** (he's good at war, which means he's good at **ending lives**)

**Personality:**
A man of few words. His demeanor is gruff, his expression serious. He's ruthless in a fight, efficient in a kill. He has a code, but it's **flexible** when the coin is right. He doesn't betray those who hire him, but he doesn't **lose sleep** over those who get in his way.

He's looking for redemption, but he doesn't **deserve it**. He knows that. He just doesn't care.

**Backstory:**
Garrick was once a soldier in a powerful army. He saw things that broke other men. He **did things** that should have broken him. But he didn't break. He **bent**. And when the bending was done, he was something new: a man who would kill for coin, but never for free.

He deserted when he realized the army was **worse than the enemy**. Now he's a mercenary, which is just a soldier without the **pretenses**. He joined this party because the mission pays well, and because he's **tired of running**.

**The Truth:**
Garrick's rapier is fast, but his nightmares are **faster**. He doesn't talk about the war. He doesn't talk about the things he did. He doesn't talk about the things that were done **to him**.

His code is simple: don't betray the client, don't trust anyone, and **never look back**. But the past has a way of catching up. And Garrick's past is **gaining on him**.

---

## **THE TRUTH ABOUT ALL OF THEM**

They are not heroes. They are **broken things** that still move.

- **Eldrin** runs from the dark, but the dark is **always behind him**.
- **Vespatian** believes in his god, but his god is **silent when it matters**.
- **Garrick** follows a code, but his code is **written in blood**.

They travel together because it's safer that way. They trust each other because they have to. But trust is a **luxury**, and luxury is for people who can afford to **die with a clear conscience**.

**Final Note:**
These are the people who will **save the world**, if it needs saving. More likely, they'll save themselves and let the world **burn around them**. Either way, the dark will still be there when they're done. And the dark is **always hungry**.
