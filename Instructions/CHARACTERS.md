// CHARACTERS: THE BROKEN AND THE DAMNED

These are not heroes. They are **survivors**. Some are better at pretending otherwise. Most are worse. All of them are carrying something that will eventually break them.

---

## **ELDRIN OF MISHARK**

Eldrin is tall and lean in the way of men who move fast and eat rarely. His hair is tied back in a braid that’s been fraying for weeks because he hasn’t had a quiet moment to fix it and won’t ask anyone to help. His eyes are the color of a forest stream, which sounds poetic until you notice they’re always moving, always measuring exits. His longbow is dark yew, worn smooth where his fingers grip. His hands are steady. His sleep isn’t.

He grew up in Mishark, where the trees watch you and the animals have **human eyes**. His father taught him the most important lesson a man can learn: **when the shadows get too long, you run**. Eldrin learned that lesson well. When his village was attacked by bandits, he didn’t stay to fight. He **ran**, like his father taught him. Now he tells himself it was to seek justice. The truth is simpler: he was afraid. He still is. But he’s better at hiding it now.

He doesn’t protect people because it’s right. He protects them because he’s **terrified** of being alone in the dark again. His arrows fly true, but his hands shake when no one’s looking. He’s not a hero. He’s a man who knows the cost of failure, and he’s **not willing to pay it again**.

---

## **VESPATIAN**

Vespatian is the kind of man who looks like he was designed to be **comforting**. Average height. Stout build. Eyes the color of warm tea, the kind that make people want to confess their sins. He wears simple robes of white and gold, the symbol of his god prominently displayed on a pendant around his neck. His hands are calloused from years of wielding a mace, but his smile is still soft. Too soft.

He believes in his god with a fervor that is **either inspiring or terrifying**, depending on who you ask. But there’s a **crack** in his faith. It’s small, but it’s there. He’s seen too many miracles that weren’t. He’s healed too many wounds that shouldn’t have been there in the first place. His kindness is real, but his certainty is **fraying at the edges**.

His healing magic leaves **black veins** creeping up his arms. He tells himself it’s the cost of service. The truth is, he doesn’t know if his god is listening anymore. He still prays. He still heals. But every time he closes his eyes, he sees the faces of those he **couldn’t save**.

His mace is heavy. His faith is **heavier**.

---
## **GARRICK THE UNBROKEN**

Garrick is tall. Muscular. The kind of man who looks like he was carved from a block of **cold, unyielding stone**. A scar runs diagonally across his face, a reminder of a battle he **shouldn’t have survived**. His dark hair falls to his shoulders, his eyes are the color of a blade in moonlight. He wears practical leather armor, reinforced with metal plates—the kind of armor that says *I expect to be hit, and I expect to hit back harder*. His rapier is finely crafted, its edge **always sharp**.

He has a code. It’s simple: don’t betray the client, don’t trust anyone, and **never look back**. But the past has a way of catching up. And Garrick’s past is **gaining on him**.

His rapier is fast, but his nightmares are **faster**. He doesn’t talk about the war. He doesn’t talk about the things he did. He doesn’t talk about the things that were done **to him**. He joined this party because the mission pays well, and because he’s **tired of running**.

He’s not a hero. He’s a man who knows how to **end lives**, and he’s good at it. But every time he closes his eyes, he sees the faces of those he’s killed. And he wonders if the coin was worth the **blood on his hands**.

---
## **MALRIK THE ASHEN**

The first time she cast a fire spell, her hands blistered. The second time, the blisters split. The third time, the skin fused to the staff. She didn’t stop. She couldn’t. The fire was the only thing that kept the dark away.

Malrik is a gaunt woman with skin like parchment stretched too tight over bone. Her hair was once dark, but now it’s streaked with gray and the faintest hint of **smoke**. Her eyes are the color of embers—bright when angry, dull when exhausted. Her hands are wrapped in blackened cloth, the fingers slightly curled from years of heat damage. She wears a long coat of heavy leather, its edges singed and its smell permanently tinged with **burning**.

Her staff is blackened oak, its tip still glowing faintly from the last spell. The air around her shimmers with heat, like the haze above a desert at noon. When she’s angry, which is often, the air **distorts**.

She doesn’t trust easily. Fire has taught her that lesson too well. She’s seen what happens when you get too close to something that burns, and she’s not interested in repeating the experience. But she’s not cruel, either. Just **cautious**. Like a woman who’s been burned too many times.

She has a dark sense of humor, the kind that makes people laugh nervously and then look away. She calls it honesty. Other people call it **disturbing**.

Her magic doesn’t just burn her enemies. It burns **her**, from the inside out. She can feel it, always, like a coal in her chest. Some days it’s a spark. Other days it’s a **wildfire**. She’s learned to control it, mostly. But control isn’t the same as **mastery**.

The fire doesn’t just scar her hands. It scars her **soul**. And some nights, when the flames dance too high, she wonders if there’s anything left of her that isn’t **ash**.

---
## **THE TRUTH ABOUT ALL OF THEM**

They travel together because the arithmetic demands it. Not trust—**arithmetic**. Four broken things that still move, held together by the shared understanding that the dark outside is worse than the dark they carry with them. For now.

Eldrin runs from the dark, but the dark is **always behind him**. Vespatian believes in his god, but his god is **silent when it matters**. Garrick follows a code, but his code is **written in blood**. Malrik controls the fire, but the fire **controls her**.

They are not heroes. They are **broken things** that still move. And when the end comes, they’ll save themselves and let the world **burn around them**. Either way, the dark will still be there. And the dark is **always hungry**.
